# Asheron's Call Gameplay Mechanics Research

## Overview
Asheron's Call was released on November 2, 1999, as the third major MMORPG. Unlike its contemporaries, it featured a unique skill-based progression system rather than traditional classes, offering unprecedented flexibility in character development.

## Character System

### Primary Attributes (6 Total)
- **Strength**: Physical power for melee damage and carrying capacity
- **Endurance**: Stamina and physical resilience
- **Coordination**: Precision for missile weapons and crafting
- **Quickness**: Attack speed and evasion
- **Focus**: Mental acuity for magic and perception skills
- **Self**: Willpower and mana capacity

### Secondary Attributes
- Health, Stamina, and Mana (derived from primary attributes)
- Could be raised directly or indirectly through primary attributes
- Used in combat, jumping, crafting, and spellcasting

### Skill System
- **Skill-based progression** - no traditional classes or level restrictions
- Players selected initial skills during character creation
- Base skill levels determined by character attributes
- Skills could be:
  - **Untrained**: Basic competency
  - **Trained**: Improved effectiveness (cost skill credits)
  - **Specialized**: Maximum effectiveness (cost 2x skill credits)
- Skills could be reallocated later in the game
- Experience points invested directly into individual skills or attributes

## Combat Mechanics

### Melee Combat
- **Speed/Power Slider**: Players could adjust between fast/weak and slow/powerful attacks
- **Attack Types**: Slash, thrust, and multi-strike attacks depending on weapon
- **Charge Mechanic**: Rapid movement toward target when attacking
- **Positional Bonuses**:
  - +10% bonus against recently hit targets
  - -20% defense penalty when flanked
- **Stance System**: Different attack heights (high/medium/low) vs enemy stances

### Missile Combat
- **Coordination-based damage** calculation
- **Speed/Accuracy Slider**: -40% accuracy at fastest speed, highest accuracy at slowest
- **Weapon Types**: Bows, crossbows, thrown weapons, atlatls
- **Ammunition Management**: Different arrow/bolt types for different effects

### Magic Combat
- **Four Schools of Magic**:
  - **War Magic**: Elemental damage (fire, ice, lightning, acid) and physical (slash, pierce, bludgeon)
  - **Life Magic**: Healing, harm, drain, and mana conversion
  - **Creature Enchantment**: Buffs/debuffs for attributes and skills
  - **Item Enchantment**: Enhance weapons, armor, and equipment
- **Seven Spell Levels**: Required skill thresholds (20/50/100/150/200/250/300)
- **Targeting**: Projectile spells required manual aim

### Advanced Combat Skills
- **Sneak Attack**: +10 (trained) to +20 (specialized) damage rating from behind
- **Deception**: Chance to trigger sneak attack damage from the front
- **Recklessness**: Increase damage output at cost of increased damage taken
- **Dirty Fighting**: Chance to inflict bleeding/weakening effects

## Magic System

### Component Systems

#### Original System (1999)
- **Complex Formula**: 5-8 components per spell in specific order
- **Component Types**:
  - Scarabs (level-specific: lead, iron, copper, silver, gold, pyreal)
  - Tapers (including 2 personal tapers unique to each player)
  - Herbs, Powders, Potions, Talismans
- **Spell Research**: Players had to discover formulas through experimentation
- **Spell Economy**: Overused spells became less effective server-wide

#### Foci System (Later)
- **Simplified Casting**: One foci per magic school + scarab + prismatic tapers
- **Foci Properties**:
  - Occupied entire pack slot
  - Could not be dropped/sold
  - Purchased from Scrivener NPCs

### Spell Economy
- More popular spells became weaker over time
- Economy rankings: Flows → Surges → Courses → Rushes → Blazes
- Example: Flame Bolt V became unusable due to overuse
- System eventually removed due to balance issues

## Tinkering & Crafting

### Salvaging
- **Process**: Destroy loot items to obtain raw materials
- **Workmanship**: Quality rating affecting tinkering success
- **Partial Bags**: Combined to create 100-unit complete bags
- **Material Types**: Different salvage for different enhancement effects

### Tinkering Applications
- **Weapon Imbuing**:
  - Sunstone: Armor Rending (ignore enemy armor)
  - Black Opal: Critical Strike (increased critical chance)
  - Fire Opal: Crippling Blow (increased critical damage)
  - Resistance Rending: Damage type vulnerabilities
- **Armor Imbuing**: +1 stackable defense bonuses
- **Limits**: Items could be tinkered 10 times, imbued only once
- **Success Rates**: Base 33% (38% with augmentation), Foolproof bags = 100%

### Other Crafting Skills
- **Alchemy**: Potions, oils, and consumables
- **Cooking**: Food buffs and stamina restoration
- **Fletching**: Arrow and bolt creation
- **Lockpicking**: Access to locked chests and doors

## World & Travel

### Portal System
- **One-way portals** scattered throughout the world
- **Portal Tying**: Bind to specific portal destinations
- **Portal Recall**: Return to tied locations
- **No Fast Travel**: Required strategic portal management

### Lifestone System
- **Death Respawn**: Players resurrect at last attuned lifestone
- **Lifestone Recall**: Teleport to attuned lifestone via command or spell
- **Lifestone Tie**: Separate spell for binding to specific lifestone
- **Evolution**: Originally Yalaini portal beacons, converted by Asheron

### Death Penalties
- **Vitae Penalty**: Temporary reduction in skills/attributes
- **Item Loss**: Drop valuable items on death (recoverable)
- **Pyreal Loss**: Lose 50% of carried currency
- **Corpse Recovery**: Required returning to death location

## Progression Systems

### Experience System
- **Multiple Sources**: Combat, quests, exploration, crafting
- **Direct Investment**: XP spent on specific skills or attributes
- **No Level Cap**: Continuous progression possible
- **Skill Point Rewards**: Additional points at certain experience milestones

### Quest System
- **Facility Hub**: Guided progression path from levels 10-50
- **Percentage-based Rewards**: Scaled to player level with caps
- **Quest Chains**: Multi-part storylines with cumulative rewards
- **Timer Systems**: Daily, weekly, and one-time quests
- **Flag System**: Prerequisite completion tracking

### Dungeon Design
- **No Instances**: All dungeons shared by all players
- **Respawn Timers**: Monsters and loot on fixed respawn cycles
- **Complexity Levels**: From simple linear to complex maze-like layouts
- **Environmental Hazards**: Pits, traps, elemental damage zones

## Social Systems

### Allegiance Hierarchy
- **Patron-Vassal Structure**: Pyramidal organization
- **Experience Passup**: Vassals generate bonus XP for patrons
  - Based on patron's Leadership skill
  - Based on vassal's Loyalty skill
  - Not deducted from vassal's earned XP
- **Rank System**:
  - Rank 1-10 based on number of followers
  - Rank 10 "High King" requires 1,022+ followers
- **Vassal Limits**: Maximum 12 direct vassals per patron

### Monarchy Features
- **Monarch Powers**: Allegiance-wide benefits and management
- **Officer Ranks**: Delegated powers for allegiance management
- **Housing Benefits**: Shared mansion recall and storage access
- **Communication**: Allegiance-wide chat channels

### Fellowship System
- **Temporary Groups**: Up to 9 players for adventuring
- **XP Sharing**: Even distribution or proportional options
- **Loot Options**: Various distribution methods
- **Fellowship Bonuses**: Increased XP for group play

## Economy

### Currency
- **Pyreals**: Primary currency from monster drops
- **Trade Notes**: Higher denomination portable currency
- **MMDs**: Massively Multiplayer Dollars (special currency)

### Trading Systems
- **Secure Trade**: Window-based player-to-player trading
- **Vendor NPCs**: Buy/sell basic equipment and components
- **Player Shops**: Automated vendor bots in houses
- **Rare System**: Unique items with limited spawn timers

## Unique Features

### Seamless World
- **No Zone Loading**: Entire surface world without loading screens
- **Real Distance**: Visible terrain was actual traversable space
- **Size**: Approximately 500 square miles of explorable terrain

### Monthly Updates
- **Live Events**: Story-driven world changes
- **New Content**: Regular addition of quests, dungeons, items
- **World Evolution**: Permanent changes based on player actions
- **Dynamic NPCs**: Characters that moved, died, or changed roles

### Housing System
- **Apartments**: Instanced personal storage
- **Cottages/Villas/Mansions**: Open-world player housing
- **Maintenance**: Monthly upkeep costs
- **Decoration**: Extensive customization options
- **Storage**: Shared chests for allegiance members

## Combat Calculations

### Attack Success
- **Formula**: Attacker Skill vs Defender Skill
- **Modifiers**:
  - Weapon enchantments (Heart Seeker, Blood Drinker)
  - Buffs/Debuffs
  - Positional advantages
  - Height advantages

### Damage Calculation
- **Base Damage**: Weapon damage range
- **Modifiers**:
  - Attribute bonuses (Strength for melee, Coordination for missile)
  - Skill level multipliers
  - Critical hits (2x-4x damage)
  - Resistance/Vulnerability

### Burden System
- **Carrying Capacity**: Based on Strength attribute
- **Encumbrance Effects**: Movement and combat penalties
- **Burden Reduction**: Special items and spells

## PvP Mechanics

### PK (Player Killer) System
- **Opt-in PvP**: Players choose to become PK or Non-PK
- **PK Lite**: Limited PvP with reduced penalties
- **Darktide Server**: Full PvP with no safe zones

### PvP Features
- **Looting**: Full inventory loot on PvP death
- **Radar**: Different colors for PK status
- **Safe Zones**: Towns with PK protection (varies by server)
- **Monarchy Wars**: Large-scale allegiance battles

## Notable Mechanics

### Sliding/Strafing
- **Combat Movement**: Slide-casting while maintaining spell aim
- **Animation Canceling**: Interrupt actions for faster response

### Jump Skill
- **Trained/Specialized**: Affected jump height and distance
- **Combat Applications**: Dodging projectiles and positioning
- **Exploration**: Access to otherwise unreachable areas

### Run Skill
- **Movement Speed**: Directly affected run speed
- **Stamina Efficiency**: Reduced stamina consumption when specialized

This comprehensive overview covers the core gameplay mechanics that made Asheron's Call unique in the MMORPG landscape, focusing on the systems and mechanics rather than lore or narrative elements.
